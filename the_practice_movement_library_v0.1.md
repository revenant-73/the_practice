It should help ZenCoder build practice cards that are more useful than vague summaries.

Each movement includes:

- Movement name
- Category
- Purpose
- How to perform
- What to notice
- Green / Yellow / Red track modifications

- Suggested metadata

The goal is not to create a giant exercise database yet.

The goal is to create a clear v0.1 movement library for:

1. Morning Practice
2. Track modifications

# 2. App Integration Rules

## 2.1 Practice Sections

Each practice section should include:

- Section title
- Duration
- Purpose statement
- Movement checklist
- Track modification box
- Optional “What to notice” prompt

## 2.2 Movement Display Format

Each movement should be displayed in the app like this:

```text
Movement Name

Why:
Short purpose statement.

How:
1–3 simple directions.

Notice:
One attention cue.

Adapt:
Green / Yellow / Red modification.
```

## 2.3 Do Not Over-Coach

The app should not give robotic technique instructions.

The language should invite attention and exploration.

Use:

- “Notice…”
- “Explore…”
- “Move slowly enough to feel…”
- “Stay below sharp pain…”
- “Find the version that feels useful today…”

Avoid:

- “Correct form”
- “Perfect position”
- “Never…”
- “Always…”
- “Fix…”

## 2.4 Track Logic

### Green Track

Use the full version.

Add controlled athletic intent.

Allow gentle power or spring if the movement supports it.

### Yellow Track

Keep the structure.

Reduce dose, range, speed, or impact.

Prioritize quality, isometrics, controlled movement, and rhythm.

### Red Track

Restore only.

No jumping, no aggressive loading, no sharp end-range forcing.

Use gentle movement, breathing, pressure shifts, and supported positions.

# 3. Morning Practice v0.1 Structure

## Section 1 — Arrive

**Duration:** 2–3 minutes  
**Purpose:** Shift from sleep/stress into awareness and movement.

Movements:

1. Diaphragmatic Breathing
2. Eye Movements
3. Head Nods + Rotations
4. Rolling: Back to Stomach / Stomach to Back

---

## Section 2 — Spine Flow

**Duration:** 3–5 minutes  
**Purpose:** Restore spinal options through flexion, extension, rotation, and side-body reach.

Movements:

1. Segmental Cat-Cow
2. Thread the Needle
3. World’s Greatest Stretch
4. Open Books
5. Child’s Pose Reaches
6. Cobra to Child’s Pose Flow

---

## Section 3 — Feet & Achilles

**Duration:** 4–6 minutes  
**Purpose:** Wake up the feet, restore foot-ground awareness, and gently load the calf/Achilles complex.

Movements:

1. Toe Yoga / Toe Spreading
2. Short-Foot Holds
3. Slow Calf Raises
4. Bent-Knee Calf Raises
5. Tibialis Raises
6. Ankle Bounces
7. Gentle Pogos

---

## Section 4 — Athletic Transitions

**Duration:** 5 minutes  
**Purpose:** Build whole-body coordination through crawling, lunging, squatting, and floor-to-standing transitions.

Movements:

1. Deep Squat Exploration
2. Reverse Lunges
3. Bear Crawl
4. Crab Walk
5. Cross-Body Crawl
6. Floor-to-Standing Transitions

**Duration:** 3–5 minutes

1. Skipping
2. Lateral Shuffle
3. Carioca
4. Single-Leg Balance
5. Quarter-Turn Jumps

**Duration:** 1 minute  
**Purpose:** Seal the practice with quiet power and reflection.

## 4.1 Diaphragmatic Breathing

**Category:** Arrive / Breathing / Recovery  
**Primary Capacities:** Downregulation, rib movement, trunk awareness  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 5 slow breaths

### Purpose

Begin the practice by noticing breath, ribs, and tension.

### How

Lie on your back with knees bent or legs relaxed.

Place one hand on your chest and one hand near your lower ribs or belly.

Breathe through your nose if possible.

Let the inhale expand the ribs and belly gently.

Let the exhale soften the body into the floor.

### Notice

Where does the breath move easily?

Where does it feel blocked?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use 5 breaths, then move on.

**Yellow:** Use 6–8 breaths and extend the exhale slightly.

**Red:** Stay here longer. This can be the main practice if needed.

### App Notes

Use this as the first card of the Morning Practice.

Do not make it sound like meditation. Keep it movement-oriented.

---

## 4.2 Eye Movements

**Category:** Arrive / Vision / Neck  
**Primary Capacities:** Visual tracking, neck relaxation, vestibular awareness  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 3–5 reps each direction

### Purpose

Wake up the visual system and reduce unnecessary neck tension.

### How

Keep the head still.

Move only the eyes:

- Left and right
- Up and down
- Diagonal up-left to down-right
- Diagonal up-right to down-left

Move slowly.

Do not force range.

### Notice

Does one direction feel harder, jumpier, or more restricted?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Perform all directions with smooth control.

**Yellow:** Reduce range and move slower.

**Red:** Use only comfortable directions. Stop if dizzy.

### App Notes

If dizziness is reported, this should be gentle or skipped.

Useful future snack: “2-Minute Eye + Neck Reset.”

---

## 4.3 Head Nods + Rotations

**Category:** Arrive / Neck / Cervical Mobility  
**Primary Capacities:** Neck range, head control, upper spine awareness  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 3–5 each direction

### Purpose

Gently check and restore neck motion.

### How

Move the head slowly:

- Nod yes
- Turn no
- Ear toward shoulder

Stay below sharp pain.

Keep the jaw soft.

### Notice

Which direction feels guarded?

Does the breath change during the movement?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use full comfortable range.

**Yellow:** Use smaller range and slower speed.

**Red:** Only micro-movements or breathing. Avoid painful directions.

### App Notes

High neck pain should trigger extra caution and a Neck Priority recommendation.

---

## 4.4 Rolling: Back to Stomach / Stomach to Back

**Category:** Arrive / Original Strength / Floor Movement  
**Primary Capacities:** Cross-body coordination, trunk rotation, vestibular system  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 3–5 rolls each direction

### Purpose

Reconnect the head, eyes, spine, ribs, and hips through a simple whole-body pattern.

### How

Start lying on your back.

Reach one arm across your body and allow the rest of the body to follow.

Roll to your stomach without forcing.

Then roll back.

Explore leading with:

- Eyes
- Head
- Arm
- Leg

### Notice

Which lead makes the roll easier?

Does one side feel more coordinated?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Explore multiple ways to initiate the roll.

**Yellow:** Move slowly and reduce effort.

**Red:** Use small partial rolls or simply rock side to side.

### App Notes

This movement is a strong bridge into Spine Flow and Athletic Transitions.

---

## 4.5 Segmental Cat-Cow

**Category:** Spine Flow / Mobility  
**Primary Capacities:** Spinal flexion, extension, segmental awareness  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 6–8 reps

### Purpose

Explore spinal flexion and extension without forcing range.

### How

Start on hands and knees.

Slowly round the spine upward.

Then slowly lengthen and extend the spine.

Try to move one region at a time instead of collapsing all at once.

### Notice

Which area moves easily?

Which area feels stuck?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Move through the full comfortable wave.

**Yellow:** Slow down and stay in controlled range.

**Red:** Use small range and pair with breathing.

### App Notes

This belongs early in Spine Flow.

---

## 4.6 Thread the Needle

**Category:** Spine Flow / Thoracic Rotation  
**Primary Capacities:** Thoracic rotation, shoulder reach, rib mobility  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 5 per side

### Purpose

Open rotation through the upper back and ribs.

### How

Start on hands and knees.

Reach one arm underneath the body and across the floor.

Let the upper back rotate.

Return and optionally reach the same arm toward the ceiling.

### Notice

Can the ribs rotate without the low back doing all the work?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Add reach toward the ceiling.

**Yellow:** Stay controlled and avoid forcing end-range.

**Red:** Use a smaller reach or perform from child’s pose.

### App Notes

If thoracic stiffness is high, recommend extra time here.

---

## 4.7 World’s Greatest Stretch

**Category:** Spine Flow / Hip Mobility / Lunge  
**Primary Capacities:** Hip extension, thoracic rotation, ankle range  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 3 per side

### Purpose

Connect hip mobility, trunk rotation, and breathing.

### How

Step into a long lunge.

Place hands inside the front foot.

Reach one arm toward the ceiling while rotating through the upper back.

Return and switch sides.

### Notice

Where do you feel the biggest limitation: hip, ankle, spine, or breath?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use full controlled lunge and reach.

**Yellow:** Shorten the stance and reduce depth.

**Red:** Use hands elevated on a couch, bench, or wall.

### App Notes

This can be hard first thing in the morning. Allow an easier version.

---

## 4.8 Open Books

**Category:** Spine Flow / Thoracic Rotation  
**Primary Capacities:** Rotation, rib mobility, shoulder opening  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 5 per side

### Purpose

Restore upper-back rotation while supported by the floor.

### How

Lie on your side with knees bent.

Stack arms in front of you.

Open the top arm across your body like opening a book.

Let the ribs rotate.

Return slowly.

### Notice

Can the breath expand into the rotated position?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use full comfortable arc.

**Yellow:** Reduce range and pause where the ribs feel restricted.

**Red:** Use smaller movement. Support the top knee with a pillow if needed.

### App Notes

Useful when neck/thoracic stiffness is high.

---

## 4.9 Child’s Pose Reaches

**Category:** Spine Flow / Side Body / Recovery  
**Primary Capacities:** Lat length, rib expansion, breathing, shoulder flexion  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 3–5 reaches each direction

### Purpose

Create space through the side body, ribs, and shoulders.

### How

Sit hips toward heels.

Reach both hands forward.

Walk hands to the left and breathe into the right side.

Walk hands to the right and breathe into the left side.

### Notice

Which side expands more easily?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use active reaches.

**Yellow:** Move slower and breathe into restriction.

**Red:** Use a pillow under the chest or skip if knees/hips dislike it.

### App Notes

Good low-threat option for Red Track.

---

## 4.10 Cobra to Child’s Pose Flow

**Category:** Spine Flow / Extension-Flexion  
**Primary Capacities:** Spinal extension, flexion, shoulder support, breathing  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 5–8 slow flows

### Purpose

Move the spine between gentle extension and recovery flexion.

### How

Begin on stomach.

Press lightly through hands into a gentle cobra.

Do not jam the low back.

Return through hands and knees into child’s pose.

Move back and forth smoothly.

### Notice

Does extension feel distributed or pinchy?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use smooth full comfortable range.

**Yellow:** Use low cobra only.

**Red:** Replace with cat-cow or child’s pose breathing.

### App Notes

If low back pain is high, reduce or replace this movement.

---

## 4.11 Toe Yoga / Toe Spreading

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Foot Awareness  
**Primary Capacities:** Toe control, arch awareness, foot dexterity  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 60 seconds

### Purpose

Wake up the small movements of the feet.

### How

Stand or sit barefoot.

Try:

- Big toe up, other toes down
- Big toe down, other toes up
- Spread all toes
- Press toes gently into floor

Do not worry if it is messy.

### Notice

Which toes are hard to access?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Perform standing.

**Yellow:** Perform seated or with hand support.

**Red:** Seated only, gentle exploration.

### App Notes

Language should normalize difficulty. This is exploration, not a pass/fail test.

---

## 4.12 Short-Foot Holds

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Arch Capacity  
**Primary Capacities:** Arch awareness, intrinsic foot strength  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 5 holds of 5 seconds

### Purpose

Explore active arch support without gripping the toes.

### How

Stand barefoot.

Keep toes relaxed.

Gently draw the ball of the foot toward the heel to lift the arch.

Hold briefly.

Relax.

### Notice

Can you lift the arch without curling the toes?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Perform standing or single-leg assisted.

**Yellow:** Perform with hand support.

**Red:** Perform seated.

### App Notes

This supports future Capacity Map: Foot Architecture.

## 4.13 Slow Calf Raises

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Tendon Capacity  
**Primary Capacities:** Calf strength, Achilles loading, ankle control  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 10 reps

### Purpose

Gently load the calf and Achilles through controlled range.

### How

Stand tall.

Rise onto the balls of the feet.

Pause briefly.

Lower slowly.

Use a wall or chair for support if needed.

### Notice

Does one side feel weaker, stiffer, or more protective?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Full controlled reps. Optional single-leg assisted.

**Yellow:** Use two legs and slow tempo.

**Red:** Use isometric holds or seated heel raises.

### App Notes

If Achilles pain is elevated, recommend isometric version before dynamic reps.

---

## 4.14 Bent-Knee Calf Raises

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Soleus Capacity  
**Primary Capacities:** Soleus strength, bent-knee ankle capacity, tendon resilience  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 10 reps

### Purpose

Load the calf/Achilles complex in a knee-bent position useful for jumping, landing, and athletic stance.

### How

Stand with knees slightly bent.

Keep the knees bent as you rise onto the balls of the feet.

Lower slowly.

Use support as needed.

### Notice

Does bent-knee loading feel different than straight-knee loading?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Full controlled reps.

**Yellow:** Reduce range and tempo.

**Red:** Use gentle isometric hold or skip if painful.

### App Notes

High relevance to volleyball movement.

## 4.15 Tibialis Raises

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Lower Leg Balance  
**Primary Capacities:** Anterior shin strength, ankle control, deceleration support  
**Equipment:** Wall optional  
**Default Dose:** 15 reps

### Purpose

Strengthen the front of the shin and balance lower-leg loading.

### How

Stand with back against a wall or free-standing.

Keep heels down.

Lift toes toward shins.

Lower slowly.

### Notice

Does the front of the shin fatigue quickly?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Full reps.

**Yellow:** Fewer reps or slower tempo.

**Red:** Gentle toe lifts while seated.

### App Notes

Useful in Achilles Priority recommendations.

## 4.16 Ankle Bounces

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Elasticity Prep  
**Primary Capacities:** Ankle rhythm, low-level elasticity, foot-ground interaction  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 20–30 seconds

### Purpose

Introduce gentle rhythm and spring without true jumping intensity.

### How

Stand tall.

Keep the movement small.

Let the ankles pulse lightly.

Heels may barely leave the floor or stay down.

### Notice

Does the movement feel springy or guarded?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Light rhythmic bounce.

**Yellow:** Smaller range and slower rhythm.

**Red:** Replace with pressure shifts.

### App Notes

If Achilles pain >= 5, app should recommend caution.

## 4.17 Gentle Pogos

**Category:** Feet & Achilles / Elasticity  
**Primary Capacities:** Elastic rebound, calf stiffness, landing rhythm  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 20 reps

### Purpose

Reintroduce spring and landing rhythm.

### How

Use small two-leg hops.

Keep knees relatively soft.

Land quietly.

Stay tall.

Stop before pain or heavy fatigue.

### Notice

Can you rebound quietly without bracing?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** 20 controlled pogos.

**Yellow:** Reduce reps or use ankle bounces instead.

**Red:** No pogos.

### App Notes

Do not include in Red Track.

For Loren, pogos should be conservative early because Achilles pain is high.

## 4.18 Deep Squat Exploration

**Category:** Athletic Transitions / Squat / Floor Access  
**Primary Capacities:** Hip, ankle, knee, spine comfort; floor access  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 60 seconds

### Purpose

Explore comfort near the floor and restore squat options.

### How

Lower into a comfortable squat.

Use support if needed.

Shift side to side.

Let the spine and hips adjust.

Stand up when ready.

### Notice

What limits the squat today: ankles, hips, back, balance, or breath?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Explore unsupported and shift directions.

**Yellow:** Use hands or a doorframe for support.

**Red:** Use a higher squat or sit-to-stand from a chair.

### App Notes

Supports long-term goal: getting to and from the floor easily.

## 4.19 Reverse Lunges

**Category:** Athletic Transitions / Single-Leg Strength  
**Primary Capacities:** Hip control, leg strength, balance, deceleration  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 5 per side

### Purpose

Prepare the hips and legs for single-leg loading.

### How

Step one foot backward.

Lower with control.

Push through the front foot to return.

Keep the movement smooth.

### Notice

Does one side feel less stable?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Full controlled lunges.

**Yellow:** Shorter step or hand support.

**Red:** Use a supported split-stance weight shift.

### App Notes

If knee pain spikes, reduce depth or replace with split-stance rocking.

## 4.20 Bear Crawl

**Category:** Athletic Transitions / Crawling  
**Primary Capacities:** Cross-body coordination, shoulder stability, trunk integration  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 20–40 seconds

### Purpose

Connect shoulders, hips, spine, and core through cross-body movement.

### How

Start on hands and feet or hands and knees.

Move opposite hand and foot together.

Keep the movement slow enough to control.

Try forward and backward.

### Notice

Can you move without holding your breath?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Hands and feet, forward/backward.

**Yellow:** Hands and knees or shorter distance.

**Red:** Quadruped rocking or crawling on hands and knees.

### App Notes

Likely high value for neck/thoracic stiffness. Future snack candidate.

## 4.21 Crab Walk

**Category:** Athletic Transitions / Posterior Chain / Shoulder Extension  
**Primary Capacities:** Shoulder extension, posterior chain, trunk support  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 20–30 seconds

### Purpose

Explore backward support, shoulder extension, and hip drive.

### How

Sit on the floor.

Place hands behind you.

Lift hips slightly.

Move hands and feet to travel or shift.

Keep shoulders comfortable.

### Notice

Do shoulders or wrists limit this more than hips?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Travel forward/backward.

**Yellow:** Use small hip lifts and weight shifts.

**Red:** Skip if wrists or shoulders complain; use seated reaches instead.

### App Notes

Because wrists/shoulders may be limiting, allow easy substitution.

## 4.22 Cross-Body Crawl

**Category:** Athletic Transitions / Coordination  
**Primary Capacities:** Cross-lateral coordination, trunk rotation, gait patterning  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 30–60 seconds

### Purpose

Reinforce opposite-side coordination.

### How

Standing or on the floor, connect opposite elbow/knee or hand/knee.

Move slowly and rhythmically.

Explore different levels:

- Standing
- Tall kneeling
- Quadruped
- Bear crawl

### Notice

Which pattern feels most coordinated?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use standing and floor variations.

**Yellow:** Stay slow and controlled.

**Red:** Use standing only, small range.

### App Notes

This is a good bridge between mobility and play.

## 4.23 Floor-to-Standing Transitions

**Category:** Athletic Transitions / Life Capacity  
**Primary Capacities:** Ground mobility, strength, coordination, confidence  
**Equipment:** Floor or mat  
**Default Dose:** 3–5 transitions

### Purpose

Practice getting down to and up from the floor in different ways.

### How

Start standing.

Get to the floor using one pathway.

Return to standing using a different pathway.

Use hands when needed.

Explore:

- Squat down
- Half-kneeling
- Side sit
- Roll to stand
- Tripod support

### Notice

Which pathway feels easiest?

Which one needs work?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Explore no-hands options if safe.

**Yellow:** Use hands and controlled tempo.

**Red:** Use supported sit-to-stand from chair or couch.

### App Notes

This is one of the most important long-term capacity movements.

## 4.32 Beautiful Vertical Jumps

**Category:** Finish / Power / Elasticity  
**Primary Capacities:** Jump coordination, landing quality, athletic intent  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** 3 jumps

### Purpose

Finish with athletic intent without chasing max effort.

### How

Stand tall.

Jump smoothly.

Land softly.

Pause after each landing.

The goal is beauty, not height.

### Notice

Did the jump feel springy, heavy, or guarded?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** 3 smooth jumps, moderate intent.

**Yellow:** 1–2 easy jumps or calf-rise reach.

**Red:** No jumps. Use a tall reach and breath.

### App Notes

The app should call these “beautiful jumps,” not “max jumps.”

This keeps the intent aligned with The Practice.

## 4.33 Quiet Landing Pause

**Category:** Finish / Landing / Deceleration  
**Primary Capacities:** Landing control, foot pressure, tendon readiness  
**Equipment:** None  
**Default Dose:** After each jump

### Purpose

Teach the body to absorb force calmly.

### How

After a jump or reach, land quietly.

Pause for one breath.

Notice foot pressure.

Stand tall before the next rep.

### Notice

Did the landing feel quiet and stable?

### Track Modifications

**Green:** Use after each jump.

**Yellow:** Use after small hops or step-downs.

**Red:** Use after standing heel raise or tall reach.

# 5. Morning Practice App Cards

## Card 1 — Arrive

**Purpose:** Shift from sleep or stress into awareness and movement.

**Movements:**

- Diaphragmatic Breathing
- Eye Movements
- Head Nods + Rotations
- Rolling

**What to Notice:**  
Where does your body feel most guarded today?

**Red Modification:**  
Stay gentle. This section can be the main practice today if needed.

## Card 2 — Spine Flow

**Duration:** 3–5 minutes

**Purpose:** Restore spinal options through flexion, extension, rotation, and side-body reach.

**Movements:**

- Segmental Cat-Cow
- Thread the Needle
- World’s Greatest Stretch
- Open Books
- Child’s Pose Reaches
- Cobra to Child’s Pose Flow

**What to Notice:**  
Where does your spine move easily, and where does it ask for patience?

**Green Modification:**  
Use smooth full-range motion and explore rhythm.

**Yellow Modification:**  
Stay controlled. Avoid forcing end-range if sharp or guarded.

**Red Modification:**  
Use small ranges, breathing, and supported positions only.

---

## Card 3 — Feet & Achilles

**Duration:** 4–6 minutes

**Purpose:** Wake up the feet, restore ground awareness, and gently load the calf/Achilles complex.

**Movements:**

- Toe Yoga / Toe Spreading
- Short-Foot Holds
- Slow Calf Raises
- Bent-Knee Calf Raises
- Tibialis Raises
- Ankle Bounces
- Gentle Pogos

**What to Notice:**  
Can your feet find the floor without gripping?

**Green Modification:**  
Use full version. Include gentle pogos if Achilles feels good.

**Yellow Modification:**  
Reduce impact. Use slow raises and isometrics. Replace pogos with ankle bounces if needed.

**Red Modification:**  
No bouncing or jumping. Use seated foot work and gentle pressure shifts.

**Yellow Modification:**

**Red Modification:**  
Use supported transitions, rocking, crawling on hands/knees, or chair-assisted movement.
