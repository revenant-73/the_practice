# The Practice — Movement Snacks Library v0.1
## Short Targeted Practices for Specific Needs

**Version:** 0.1  
**Status:** Working content library  
**Purpose:** Provide app-ready content for short movement practices that can be used outside the full Morning Practice or Strength Practice.

---

# 1. Purpose

Movement Snacks are short, targeted practices designed to help the user respond to a specific need in the moment.

They are not workouts.  
They are not corrective exercises.  
They are small opportunities to notice, adapt, and return to the day with more movement options.

Movement Snacks should answer one question:

> What is the smallest useful movement practice I can do right now?

---

# 2. App Design Principles

- **Low friction:** user should be able to start in under 10 seconds.
- **Specific need:** each snack solves a clear moment.
- **Adaptable dose:** Standard / Gentle / Build, or Green / Yellow / Red when connected to readiness.
- **No over-coaching:** invite noticing and exploration.
- **Save lightweight feedback:** what changed?

---

## Snack 1 — Neck + Thoracic Reset

**Duration:** 2–5 minutes  
**Category:** Neck + Thoracic  
**Best Used When:** Neck stiffness, upper-back stiffness, computer work, long coaching days.  

### Purpose

Reduce guarding through the neck and restore gentle movement options through the eyes, head, ribs, and upper back.

### Movements


- Diaphragmatic Breathing — 3 slow breaths
- Eye Movements — left/right, up/down, diagonals
- Head Nods + Rotations — small comfortable range
- Thread the Needle — 3 per side
- Open Books — 3 per side


### What to Notice

Does the neck soften when the eyes, ribs, and upper back start moving?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Use full comfortable range and smooth transitions.

**Yellow / Standard:** Use smaller range, slower pace, and extra breathing.

**Red / Gentle:** Use breathing, eye movements, and micro head movements only. Skip floor rotation if symptoms increase.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when neck pain >= 5, neck stiffness >= 6, thoracic stiffness >= 6, long computer day, or long coaching day.

> What changed?

## Snack 2 — Achilles Isometric Reset

**Duration:** 2–5 minutes  
**Category:** Feet + Achilles  
**Best Used When:** Achilles feels stiff, tender, or grumpy but not sharp.  

### Purpose

Give the Achilles/calf complex a calm strength signal without bouncing or aggressive loading.

### Movements


- Supported Double-Leg Calf Raise Hold — 20–30 seconds
- Bent-Knee Calf Raise Hold — 20–30 seconds
- Tibialis Raises — 10–15 reps
- Gentle Ankle Pressure Shifts — 30 seconds


### What to Notice

Does the Achilles feel calmer, warmer, or more available after the holds?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Add one round of slow calf raises after the holds.

**Yellow / Standard:** Use isometrics only. Keep effort moderate.

**Red / Gentle:** Use seated heel raises, ankle pumps, or skip loading if pain feels unsafe.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when Achilles pain is 3–7, Achilles stiffness >= 6, or jumping has been reduced.

### Finish Prompt

> What changed?

## Snack 3 — Feet Wake-Up

**Duration:** 2–5 minutes  
**Category:** Feet + Achilles  
**Best Used When:** Feet feel stiff, arches feel collapsed, balance feels poor, or the user has worn shoes all day.  

### Purpose

Improve foot awareness, arch activity, and ground connection.

### Movements


- Barefoot Standing Pressure Scan — 30 seconds
- Toe Yoga / Toe Spreading — 60 seconds
- Short-Foot Holds — 5 holds
- Single-Leg Balance or Supported Balance — 20 seconds per side
- Gentle Barefoot Walking — 30–60 seconds


### What to Notice

Can the feet find the floor without gripping?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Add single-leg balance with gentle reaches.

**Yellow / Standard:** Use fingertip support and slower transitions.

**Red / Gentle:** Do seated toe work and gentle pressure shifts only.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when feet stiffness >= 6, Achilles stiffness elevated, or Morning Practice is selected.

### Finish Prompt

> What changed?

## Snack 4 — Low Back Decompression

**Duration:** 5–10 minutes  
**Category:** Hips + Low Back  
**Best Used When:** Low back feels stiff, compressed, or guarded.  

### Purpose

Reduce low-back guarding by restoring breathing, hips, and gentle spinal motion.

### Movements


- Supine Breathing — 5 breaths
- Knees-to-Chest Rocking — 30–60 seconds
- Cat-Cow — 6 slow reps
- Child’s Pose Reaches — 3 each direction
- 90/90 Hip Switches or Windshield Wipers — 5 each side


### What to Notice

Does the back prefer flexion, extension, rotation, or stillness today?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Add gentle hip transitions and squat exploration.

**Yellow / Standard:** Use smaller ranges and more breathing.

**Red / Gentle:** Stay on the floor. Use breathing, rocking, and supported positions.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when lower-back pain >= 4, lower-back stiffness >= 6, long standing day, or long computer day.

### Finish Prompt

> What changed?

## Snack 6 — Coach Between-Practice Reset

**Duration:** 3–7 minutes  
**Category:** Volleyball / Coaching  
**Best Used When:** Between practices, clinics, matches, or long gym blocks.  

### Purpose

Undo accumulated coaching stiffness and restore enough energy to stay present and useful.

### Movements


- Standing Breath Reset — 3 breaths
- Neck + Eye Scan — 30 seconds
- Thoracic Rotation Reach — 3 per side
- Calf Raise + Reach — 5 reps
- Lateral Weight Shifts — 30 seconds
- Easy Defensive Stance Shift — 30 seconds


### What to Notice

Am I more available for the next group?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Add light skips or shuffles if energy needs to rise.

**Yellow / Standard:** Keep it standing and rhythmic.

**Red / Gentle:** Breathing, gentle neck motion, and slow weight shifts only.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when long coaching day, tournament day, or stiffness accumulates during gym time.

Do I feel less folded forward?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Add a brisk walk or light shuffles.

**Yellow / Standard:** Use slow, controlled movement.

**Red / Gentle:** Use breathing, standing support, and gentle walking only.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when long computer day, neck stiffness >= 5, thoracic stiffness >= 5, or low-back stiffness >= 5.

### App Recommendation Logic

## Snack 9 — Downshift / Recovery

**Duration:** 5–10 minutes  
**Category:** Recovery / Restore  
**Best Used When:** High stress, post-practice, before bed, or nervous system feels elevated.  

### Purpose

Help the body shift from output mode into recovery mode.

### Movements


- Supine Breathing — 6–10 breaths
- Legs on Couch or Wall — 2 minutes
- Gentle Open Books — 3 per side
- Child’s Pose Breathing or Supported Forward Fold — 3–5 breaths
- Final Body Scan — 30 seconds


### What to Notice

What feels quieter now?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Use after hard training or late practice.

**Yellow / Standard:** Use longer exhales and supported positions.

**Red / Gentle:** This may be the entire practice today.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when stress >= 7, sleep quality <= 4, post-practice, post-strength, or Red Track.

### Finish Prompt

> What changed?

## Snack 10 — Pre-Volleyball Warm-Up Snack

**Duration:** 5–10 minutes  
**Category:** Volleyball / Pre-Practice  
**Best Used When:** Before coaching, demonstrating, playing, or entering the gym cold.  

### Purpose

Prepare the body for volleyball-specific movement without wasting energy.

### Movements


- Standing Breath + Scan — 3 breaths
- Feet Wake-Up — 60 seconds
- Spine Rotation Reach — 3 each side
- Calf Raises — 10 reps
- Lateral Shuffle — 30 seconds
- Shadow Approach — 3 reps
- Blocking Footwork or Defensive Shift — 3 reps


### What to Notice

Do I feel ready to enter volleyball movement?

### Track Adaptations

**Green / Build:** Add gentle jumps to approaches and block footwork.

**Yellow / Standard:** Footwork only. Reduce jumping.

**Red / Gentle:** No jumping. Use walking patterns, reaches, and gentle stance shifts.

### App Recommendation Logic

Recommend when volleyball practice, volleyball playing, long coaching day, or user selects prepare to coach/play.

# 5. MVP Snack Screen Design

## 5.1 Snack Home Screen

Display:

```text
Movement Snacks

What do you need right now?

[Neck + Thoracic]
[Feet + Achilles]
[Low Back]
[Hips]
[Energy]
[Recovery]
[Coaching]
[Computer Day]
[Volleyball]
```

Then show snack cards.

## 5.2 Snack Card Preview

Each snack card should show:

- Name
- Duration
- Category
- Best used when
- Intensity
- Start button

Example:

```text
Neck + Thoracic Reset
2–5 min
Best when: neck or upper back feels stiff
[Start Snack]
```

## 5.3 Snack Detail Screen

Each snack detail should show:

- Purpose
- Best used when
- Movement checklist
- What to notice
- Track/adaptation guidance
- Finish prompt
- Complete Snack button

For snacks, use **Standard / Gentle / Build** unless the user is coming from a readiness recommendation.

If coming from a readiness recommendation, use the actual Green / Yellow / Red track.

# 6. Snack Completion Reflection

After a snack, ask one quick question:

```text
What changed?
```

Optional quick buttons:

- Neck freer
- Feet better
- Achilles calmer
- Back easier
- Energy up
- Stress down
- No change
- Worse

Optional note field:

```text
Anything worth remembering?
```

Save snack completion with:

- Date/time
- Snack ID
- Duration
- Track/adaptation used
- Quick result
- Note
